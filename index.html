What is the conference all about?

Functional materials represent a fast growing set of advanced materials and composites, some properties (shape, electrical conductivity, mechanical properties, color etc.) are responsive to external stimuli (thermal, electrical, mechanical, light etc.). Recent efforts are focused on ongoing innovation and development of higher performance products on functional textiles. The use of it in a medical context includes super-hydrophobic materials, antibacterial textiles and insect-repellent materials and along with ultraviolet light protection of clothing and flame retardant textiles. In addition, materials include inorganic, organic, hybrid and nano materials, soft matter and interfaces with specific functions such as adaptive materials, electronic materials, magnetic materials, and optical materials.



About

About the College

The College of Engineering & Technology (CET), Bhubaneswar was established by the Government of Odisha in the year 1981 to meet the growing technical man-power need of the state. It is an autonomous and constituent college of Biju Patnaik University of Technology (BPUT), Odisha. The college was accredited with ‘A’ grade by the NAAC peer team. In addition, the college is also the recipient of RUSA (Rastriya Uchhatar Shikshya Abhiyan) and TEQIP-III schemes of MHRD, Govt. of India. The college is located in the Techno campus at Kalinga Nagar, near Khandagiri, Bhubaneswar.

About the Department

Chemistry: The department offers Master of Science (2-year) in Applied Chemistry and Integrated M.Sc. (5-year) in Applied Chemistry. The department has the laboratories with equipments and softwares to pursue interdisciplinary research activities including some advance materials like electronic storage devices, nanomaterials, sensors for metal ions etc.

Textile Engineering: The department offers B.Tech in Textile Engineering & M.Tech in Textile Chemical Processing programs.The department has laboratories with equipments to pursue research in textile and its allied fields.

Destinations to visit

Dhauli Shanti Stupa

Shanti stupa is a Buddhist Peace Pagoda and is the main attraction for people who believe in Lord Buddha or appreciate detailed artworks or carvings. This is a big white building covered with the dome on top and beautified with intricate carvings on it.

Udayagiri and Khandagiri Caves

One of the earliest groups of Jain rock-cut shelters, the caves of Udayagiri (Hill of Sunrise) and Khandagiri (Broken Hills) command a unique position in the filed of history, rock-cut architecture, art and religion.

Nandankanan Zoological Park

The Nandankanan Zoological Park is also known as Nandankanan National Park where arrays of enthralling facility like boating, white tiger and lion safaris (safari in a sense to encounter these royal animals) reptile park, aerial ropeway and cable car at the zoological park which is a nocturnal animal house established for the wildlife lovers to get a penchant for wildlife.

Chilika Lake

Chilika is the largest brackish or salt water lake of india in the state of odisha. It's famous for its rich biodiversity in bird species.Chilika is recognized as one of the most important wetlands in the world because it is home to a phenomenal variety of birds.

Lingaraja Temple, Bhubaneswar

Lingaraja Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to Shiva and is one of the oldest temples in Bhubaneswar, the capital of the Indian state of Odisha.

Sun temple, Konark

The Konark Sun Temple is a 13th-century Hindu temple dedicated to the Sun God.The temple is unique in its architecture and is built in the form of a chariot driven by seven horses on 12 pairs of decorated wheels.

Jagannath Temple, Puri

The Shree Jagannath Temple of Puri is an important Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Jagannath, a form of lord Maha Vishnu , located on the eastern coast of India, at Puri in the state of Odisha.
